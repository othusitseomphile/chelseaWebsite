History of Chelsea FC Since 1905

Founded in 1905, Chelsea FC has grown from a local club to a global football institution with a rich legacy.

Chelsea Football Club, founded on March 10, 1905, at The Rising Sun pub in Fulham, London, initially competed in the Football League and won their first major trophy, the First Division title, in 1955 under manager Ted Drake, followed by a flair-filled 1960s era with stars like Peter Osgood leading to the FA Cup in 1970 and the League Cup in 1965, establishing Stamford Bridge as a hub of excitement; the 1970s and 1980s brought financial difficulties and relegation battles, but the 1990s marked a resurgence with players like Gianfranco Zola and manager Ruud Gullit, securing the FA Cup in 1997 and the League Cup in 1998; the Roman Abramovich era, beginning in 2003, transformed Chelsea into a powerhouse under managers like José Mourinho, Carlo Ancelotti, and Thomas Tuchel, winning five Premier League titles, two Champions League trophies (2012, 2021), two Europa League titles, and multiple domestic cups, with the 2012 Champions League final won on penalties against Bayern Munich standing as a defining moment; the women’s team, formed in 1992, has dominated the WSL with seven league titles, while the club’s academy, among the world’s best, has produced stars like John Terry and Reece James, and global tours have built a 500-million-strong supporter base, with Stamford Bridge expanding over decades and Chelsea’s commitment to social causes like anti-discrimination campaigns and youth programs reflecting their ethos of excellence on and off the pitch.
